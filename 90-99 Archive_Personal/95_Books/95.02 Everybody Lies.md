# Everybody Lies

**Author**: Seth Stephens-Davidowitz

**Subtitle**: What the Internet Can Tell Us About Who We Really Are

Data science book exploring human behavior through internet search data, revealing insights about society that traditional surveys miss.

## Key Topics

| Topic | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| Big data analysis | Mining Google search trends for behavioral insights |
| Social science | Understanding human psychology through digital footprints |
| Data-driven research | Using unconventional data sources for research |
| Behavioral patterns | Revealed preferences vs stated preferences |
| Digital epidemiology | Tracking real-time trends and attitudes |

## Key Insights

- **Search data reveals truth** - People are more honest with search engines than surveys
- **Unconventional data sources** - Text analysis, clickstreams, browsing patterns
- **A/B testing limitations** - When experiments can mislead vs enlighten
- **Predictive analytics** - Using digital traces to forecast behavior
- **Ethical considerations** - Privacy and responsible data use

> [!TIP] Relevance for Data Scientists
> Demonstrates creative approaches to data collection and the importance of understanding revealed vs stated preferences in modeling.
