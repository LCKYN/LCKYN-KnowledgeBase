# 11.30 Scaling Laws

## Overview
**Scaling laws** describe predictable power-law relationships between LLM performance (loss) and three key resources: **model parameters (N)**, **dataset size (D)**, and **compute budget (C)**. They guide how to allocate compute optimally when training large models.

## Key Concepts
- **Power-law scaling**: Test loss decreases predictably as $L(N, D) \propto N^{-\alpha} + D^{-\beta}$
- **Compute-optimal training**: For a fixed compute budget $C \approx 6ND$, there is an optimal $(N^*, D^*)$ that minimizes loss
- **Bottleneck behavior**: Scaling only one of $N$ or $D$ yields diminishing returns; both must scale together
- **Emergent capabilities**: Some abilities appear discontinuously at scale, beyond what loss curves predict

## Kaplan vs Chinchilla

| Aspect | Kaplan et al. (2020) | Chinchilla (Hoffmann et al., 2022) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Optimal allocation | Favor larger models, less data | Equal scaling of $N$ and $D$ |
| Tokens per parameter | ~2 | **~20** |
| Implication | GPT-3 (175B / 300B tokens) | Chinchilla (70B / 1.4T tokens) outperforms GPT-3 |
| Status | Superseded | Current consensus baseline |

> [!INFO] Chinchilla Rule of Thumb
> For compute-optimal training, train on **~20 tokens per parameter**. A 7B model wants ~140B tokens; a 70B model wants ~1.4T tokens.

## Compute Budget Math
- Training FLOPs ≈ $6 \cdot N \cdot D$ (forward + backward)
- Given budget $C$: solve for $N^* \propto C^{0.5}$ and $D^* \propto C^{0.5}$
- Doubling compute → roughly $\sqrt{2}\times$ params **and** $\sqrt{2}\times$ tokens

## Practical Use Cases
- **Pre-training planning**: Sizing model vs dataset for a fixed GPU budget
- **Inference-aware training**: Modern practice (LLaMA, Qwen) intentionally **over-trains smaller models** past Chinchilla-optimal to reduce inference cost
- **Forecasting**: Predicting loss at larger scales before committing compute
- **Data sufficiency checks**: Detecting when you're token-starved vs parameter-starved

> [!TIP] Inference-Optimal ≠ Compute-Optimal
> Chinchilla minimizes **training** loss per FLOP. If the model will serve billions of tokens, train a smaller model on far more data (LLaMA-3 8B trained on 15T tokens — ~1875 tokens/param).

> [!WARNING] Limits of Scaling Laws
> - Derived under fixed architecture/optimizer assumptions
> - Don't predict emergent abilities or downstream task performance directly
> - Data quality and repetition effects can break the curve (see data-constrained scaling)
