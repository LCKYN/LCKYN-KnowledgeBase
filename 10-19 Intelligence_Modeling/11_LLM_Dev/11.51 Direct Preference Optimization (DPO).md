# Direct Preference Optimization (DPO)

## Overview
**Direct Preference Optimization (DPO)** aligns a model to human preferences *without reinforcement learning*. The key insight: the RLHF objective (maximize reward under a KL constraint) has a closed-form solution, so the reward model can be expressed in terms of the policy itself — "your language model is secretly a reward model." Preference pairs are then used directly in a simple classification-style loss.

> [!INFO] What it removes vs [[11.49 RLHF]]
> No reward model training, no PPO, no value/critic model, no on-policy sampling. Just the policy and a frozen reference model, trained offline on preference pairs — roughly the cost and complexity of [[11.31 Supervised Fine-Tuning]].

## The Loss (intuition)

For each preference pair (chosen $y_w$, rejected $y_l$):

$$\mathcal{L} = -\log \sigma\left(\beta \log \frac{\pi(y_w)}{\pi_{ref}(y_w)} - \beta \log \frac{\pi(y_l)}{\pi_{ref}(y_l)}\right)$$

- Increase the chosen response's likelihood *relative to the reference*, decrease the rejected one's
- **β** controls how far the policy may drift from the reference (the implicit KL penalty)
- The "implicit reward" is the log-probability ratio — no separate scalar RM ever exists

## DPO vs PPO-based RLHF

| | **DPO** | **PPO RLHF** |
|---|---|---|
| Models in memory | 2 (policy + frozen ref) | 4 (policy, ref, RM, critic) |
| Training data | Offline preference pairs | Online generations, fresh each step |
| Stability | Stable, few hyperparameters | Notoriously finicky |
| Exploration | None — limited to preference-data distribution | Policy explores; can exceed data quality |
| Compute | ~SFT-level | Many times SFT |
| Typical ceiling | Excellent for most applied alignment | Higher at frontier scale / with online feedback |

## Known Weaknesses

> [!WARNING] Pitfalls
> - **Likelihood displacement** — DPO can lower the probability of *both* responses (chosen just falls slower); can hurt if chosen answers were already good
> - **Overfitting to pairs** — small preference sets → the model learns surface patterns of "chosen" style
> - **Off-policy gap** — pairs generated by another model may not reflect your policy's actual failure modes; iterated DPO (regenerate pairs from the current policy) helps

## Variants

- **IPO** — bounded objective that resists overfitting when preferences are near-deterministic
- **KTO** — works with *unpaired* thumbs-up/down labels instead of pairs (cheaper data)
- **ORPO** — folds preference optimization into SFT, no reference model needed
- **SimPO** — reference-free, length-normalized implicit reward

## Practical Use Cases

- Default choice for applied preference tuning (chat quality, tone, refusal behavior, format compliance)
- Distilling a stronger model's judgments: generate pairs, rank with the strong model, DPO the weak one
- Often combined with [[11.32 PEFT]] (LoRA-DPO) to align on a single GPU
